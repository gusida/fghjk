Police violence complaints in 2019

Graphic showing the number of reports of police violence not convicted (grey) and convicted (orange) in 2019. Out of 834 cases in total, 576 cases were closed with no investigation, 250 cases investigated but not prosecuted, 7 cases acquitted in court and one convicted.

Reports of police violence: 2009-2013 compared to 2015-2019

Graphics comparing the numbers of police violence complaints (grey) and convictions (orange). In the years 2009-2013, there were between 709 and 891 reports per year from which between four and nine were convicted. In the years 2015-2019, the annual number of reports varied between 654 and 1083, from which between three and nine were convicted each year.

Graphic: Authors.
Data source: Åklagarmyndigheten / The Swedish Prosecution Authority (download the raw data here),
district court documents and the UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights.

Number of people shot dead by Swedish police

Line graph (orange) showing the number of lethal shootings by police officers per year for the time frame 1990 - 2019. Between 1990 and 2012 there were between 0 and 1 deaths per year in most cases, with an average of 0,7. Since 2013, the numbers are higher: between 2013 and 2016 there were 3 people fatally shot by police per year on average, and 2018 showed a new peak with 6 deaths that year. In 2017 and 2019, the figure was down to 1. The last year without any death from police shootings was 2012.

Graphic: Authors.
Data source: Polismyndighetens Nationella operativa avdelningen / The Swedish Police Authority.
